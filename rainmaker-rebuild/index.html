Seasonal Fishing Guide

One of our most commonly asked questions is, when is the best time to go fishing? While almost any time is a good time to go fishing, the real answer depends on what type of fish you're looking to catch.

The following is a summary of a typical season aboard the RAINMAKER IV. Use this as a guide when planning your charter.

MAY

May starts with a bang, bringing great catches for the first 15 trips. Mid-May brings King and Steelhead action along with the Coho's.

JUNE

June typically starts with an average of 20 fish per trip. Mid-month brings Northeast winds, adding more excitement to the fishing experience. This is great for the hardcore fisherman. Although waters can be rough, the fishing is good.

JULY

July comes in like a lamb but goes out like a lion. The Kings start hooking up early in the month and are heavy by month's end. This month also brings a two-week period of good sized Steelheads and nice catches of Brown Trout.

AUGUST

August is a full solid month of fishing. Fishing at sunrise brings some big four-year-old Kings. These Kings stage about four miles out in the lake, getting ready to spawn well into the Fall. This gives us an opportunity to catch fish in the 20lb class. August is also a good month for spawning Cohos.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about your fishing charter experience

Anyone over the age of 18 can book a charter. Fishing charters are booked for a variety of reasons:

  • Love of fishing
  • Fresh catch for dinner
  • Show off your big catch to your friends
  • Get out of the office
  • Treat clients and employees
  • Bachelor/bachelorette parties
  • Gifts for Father's Day, Mother's Day, birthdays, anniversaries, Christmas and other holidays (gift certificates available)

Booking your fishing charter with a professional and experienced Captain makes for a great trip.

  • First of all, both your boat and Captain are licensed and insured.
  • All safety equipment and the latest fishing gear are aboard.
  • With over 30 years of experience on Lake Michigan your Captain can put you in the right place, at the right time for the best fishing. He is familiar with the lake and networks with other Captains regularly for updates on weather and fishing.

No fishing experience, no problemâ€¦many beginners have reeled in the "big one". There are no age limitations! Your captain is more than happy to give instructions and pass on tips as to the best way to reel in the fish. Anyone can do it.

Depending on the weather, the following items are recommended:

  • Proper fishing license/salmon-trout stamp
  • Soft-soled shoes
  • Warm clothing/rain coat (if necessary)
  • Food and beverage
  • Sun glasses/sun-screen & lotion
  • Camera
  • Cooler to transport fish home

If you are of legal drinking age, you may bring alcoholic beverages on the boat. Illegal substances of any kind are not allowed.

A fishing license is required for any individual 16 and older. These licenses may be purchased prior to the trip, or your captain has a one-day license for both IL and WI available for purchase on the boat. A Wisconsin one-day license is $14.00, and the Illinois license is $6.00.

Coast guard regulations limit the number of individuals fishing to six, no matter what the age.

Per regulation, a limit catch for each person on the boat is five fish. A limit catch is always the captain's goal.

On Rainmaker charters, you'll have the opportunity to catch a variety of species including:

  • King Salmon
  • Coho Salmon
  • Steelhead
  • Lake Trout
  • Brown Trout

Refer to our Best Fishing Times section for information on when each species is at its peak availability.

The Rainmaker fishes in all kinds of weather, but safety comes first. If weather reports are unfavorable, the captain may call you the night before to let you know there is a chance the charter may need to be rescheduled. He will contact you again in the morning with the final decision. Although cancellations happen, they are rare. Your deposit is never lost due to weather related cancellations.

The Captain offers complete fish cleaning services at a charge of $1.00 per fish. This ensures your catch is properly prepared and ready to take home for a delicious meal.
