BEST FISHING TIMES

Lake Michigan offers excellent fishing throughout the season, but knowing when to target specific species can dramatically improve your chances of success. Here's our guide to the best fishing times in Kenosha.

SPRING (April-May)

Spring brings excellent action for Brown Trout in shallow water, with Coho Salmon becoming increasingly active. Steelhead are also available with peak action in May.

Coho Salmon - Peak in May
King Salmon - Slow in April, Available in May
Brown Trout - Peak
Steelhead - Peak in May

SUMMER (June-August)

Summer offers the most diverse fishing opportunities with all species active. July brings the start of King Salmon action, while Coho remain strong. Lake Trout move deeper and provide consistent action.

Coho Salmon - Peak all summer
King Salmon - Peak in July/August
Lake Trout - Peak all summer
Steelhead - Peak in summer

FALL (September-October)

Fall is prime time for trophy King Salmon as they stage for spawning. This is when the largest Kings of the year are typically caught. Steelhead and Brown Trout action improves as water cools.

Coho Salmon - Available
King Salmon - Peak (largest fish)
Lake Trout - Good in September
Steelhead - Good in October

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Who can book a charter?

Anyone over the age of 18 can book a charter. Fishing charters are booked for a variety of reasons including love of fishing, fresh catch for dinner, corporate events, bachelor/bachelorette parties, and as gifts for special occasions like Father's Day, birthdays, or anniversaries (gift certificates available).

Why book a professional fishing charter?

Booking with a professional captain ensures your safety and success. Both your boat and Captain are licensed and insured with all safety equipment and the latest fishing gear aboard. With over 30 years of experience on Lake Michigan, Captain Al can put you in the right place at the right time for the best fishing, and networks with other Captains regularly for updates on weather and fishing conditions.

What if I've never fished before?

No fishing experience, no problem! Many beginners have reeled in the "big one" with us. There are no age limitations, and your captain is more than happy to give instructions and pass on tips for the best way to reel in the fish. Anyone can do it and have a great time!

What should I bring?

You should bring: proper fishing license/salmon-trout stamp (can be purchased on the boat if needed), soft-soled shoes, weather-appropriate clothing/rain coat, food and beverages, sunglasses/sunscreen, camera, and a cooler to transport your catch home.

Can I bring alcohol on the boat?

If you are of legal drinking age, you may bring alcoholic beverages on the boat. However, illegal substances of any kind are not allowed.

Do I need to buy a license?

A fishing license is required for any individual 16 and older. These licenses may be purchased prior to the trip, or your captain has one-day licenses for both IL and WI available for purchase on the boat. A Wisconsin one-day license is $14.00, and the Illinois license is $6.00.

How many people can fish on the boat?

Coast Guard regulations limit the number of individuals fishing to six, no matter what the age.

What is a limit catch?

Per regulation, a limit catch for each person on the boat is five fish. A limit catch is always the captain's goal.

What if the weather is bad?

We fish in all kinds of weather, but safety comes first. If weather reports are unfavorable, the captain may call you the night before to let you know there is a chance the charter may need to be rescheduled. He will contact you again in the morning with the final decision. Although cancellations happen, they are rare. Your deposit is never lost due to weather-related cancellations.

Who's going to clean the fish?

Captain Al offers complete fish cleaning services at a charge of $1.00 per fish.
